WIAL Platform Guide

Everything you need to run your chapter, manage coaches, and publish content on the World Institute for Action Learning platform.

Welcome

This guide helps you get started with the WIAL platform — a multi-tenant website that gives every WIAL chapter its own branded microsite while maintaining a shared global standard for certification, coach discovery, and content.

Whether you are a platform administrator overseeing the whole network, a chapter lead managing your local site, or a coach maintaining your certification profile, this guide walks you through every feature step by step.

Platform Overview

The WIAL platform is organized around four main areas:

Public Website

Marketing pages, coach directory, certification info, and the AI chatbot — visible to everyone.

Account Area

Personal profile, coach registration, certifications, and payment dues for authenticated users.

Chapter Admin

Content editor, event management, coach approvals, settings, and revenue for chapter leads.

Global Admin

Chapter provisioning, cross-network coach approvals, and network-wide metrics for platform admins.

Signing In

Navigate to /login and enter your email and password. After signing in, you will be redirected to the appropriate dashboard for your role:

  • Platform admin → Global admin dashboard
  • Chapter admin → Chapter content workspace
  • Content creator → Chapter content workspace
  • Coach → Coach directory profile
  • Public visitor → Coach registration page

If you do not have an account, visit /register to create one. New accounts start with the public visitor role.

Understanding Roles

The platform uses a five-tier role system. Each role unlocks a specific set of capabilities:

RoleWho is this?Key capabilities
Platform admin WIAL headquarters staff Provision chapters, approve coaches globally, manage the entire network
Chapter admin Country or regional chapter leads Edit chapter website, manage events, approve local coaches, view revenue
Content creator Assigned content editors Edit chapter pages and events (no coach approvals or settings)
Coach Certified Action Learning coaches Manage directory profile, view certifications, pay dues
Public visitor Anyone who creates an account Browse directory, view certifications, register as coach
Role progression

Users can self-promote through certain paths: Public visitor → Coach (via registration) → Chapter admin (via chapter head registration, once assigned to a chapter by a platform admin).

Global Dashboard

As a platform admin, your home screen at /admin/global shows network-wide metrics:

  • Active chapters — number of published chapter sites
  • Total chapters — all chapters including drafts
  • Pending approvals — coaches waiting for review across the network

Quick-action links let you jump directly to chapter management or the approvals queue.

Adding a New Chapter

This is one of the most important workflows in the platform. Each chapter gets its own subdomain (e.g., usa.wial.org) and a full set of web pages.

Step 1: Open the provisioning form

Navigate to /admin/global/chapters and click "Add new chapter", or go directly to /admin/global/chapters/new.

Step 2: Fill out the chapter details

  • Chapter name — e.g., "WIAL Japan". The subdomain is auto-generated from this (e.g., japan).
  • Region — select from Americas, Europe, Asia-Pacific, Africa, or Middle East.
  • Country — the chapter’s home country.
  • Language — primary language for content (English, Spanish, Portuguese, or French).
  • Chapter lead email — email of the person who will manage this chapter. They will be invited or have their role upgraded automatically.
  • Contact email & phone — public contact info shown on the chapter site.
  • Description — a short blurb about the chapter.

Step 3: Submit

When you submit the form, the system automatically:

  1. Creates the chapter record with its unique subdomain
  2. Seeds six default pages: About, Team, Events, Resources, Testimonials, and Contact
  3. Invites or assigns the chapter lead user
  4. Sends a welcome email to the chapter lead (if Resend API is configured)
  5. Returns the chapter’s public URL
After provisioning

The chapter lead can immediately sign in and start editing their pages. If DNS is not yet configured for the subdomain, the chapter is accessible via the middleware rewrite on localhost during development.

Managing Chapters

The chapters list at /admin/global/chapters shows every chapter with:

  • Name and subdomain
  • Country, language, and region
  • Status (active, draft, or inactive)

From here you can also assign content creators to any chapter by entering their email address. Content creators can edit pages and events but cannot manage coach approvals or chapter settings.

Coach Approvals (Global)

At /admin/approvals, you see every pending coach submission across the entire network. For each coach you can:

  • Approve — the coach profile is published to the directory, search embeddings are regenerated, and the coach directory is re-indexed.
  • Reject — enter an optional reason. The coach receives a rejection notification email and their profile remains unpublished.

Assigning Content Creators

Platform admins can assign any user as a content creator for a specific chapter. This is done from the chapter list at /admin/global/chapters. Content creators get access to:

  • Page editing (rich text and AI generation)
  • Event management

They do not have access to coach approvals, chapter settings, or revenue dashboards.

Your Chapter Workspace

Chapter admins and content creators work from /admin/chapter. This is your central workspace for managing your chapter’s microsite. The workspace shows all chapter pages in a list, and each page can be opened for inline editing with a live preview.

Accessing your workspace

You must either be on your chapter’s subdomain (e.g., usa.localhost:3000) or be assigned to a chapter by a platform admin. If you see "No chapter workspace selected," contact your WIAL platform administrator.

Editing Website Pages

Each chapter starts with six pre-built pages. To edit a page:

  1. Go to /admin/chapter
  2. Click on the page you want to edit
  3. The editor opens with two views:
    • Rich text editor — a structured JSON schema with hero intro, metrics, and content sections
    • HTML source view — direct access to the page HTML
  4. Make your changes
  5. Toggle the Published switch to make the page live
  6. Save your work

Content section types

Pages are built from modular content sections. The available types are:

Prose

Paragraphs and bullet lists for narrative content.

Feature Grid

Cards highlighting capabilities or benefits.

Timeline

Chronological milestones or history.

Quote

Highlighted quote with attribution.

Resource List

Downloadable files and links.

Contact Cards

Email, address, and topic cards.

Logo Grid

Partner or client logos.

Media & Prose

Image or video alongside text.

Call to Action

CTA with heading, body text, and a button link.

AI Content Generation

The platform includes an AI content assistant that can generate or rewrite page content for your chapter. This is available to platform admins and chapter admins.

How to use it

  1. Open a page in the editor
  2. Click the AI Generate button
  3. Configure the generation settings:
    • Tone — formal, conversational, inspirational, etc.
    • Include coaches — pulls in your approved coaches to feature on the page
    • Include events — pulls in upcoming events
    • Custom context — any specific information or messaging you want included
    • Testimonials — paste in testimonials to weave into the generated content
    • Language — English, Spanish, Portuguese, or French
  4. Click generate
  5. Review and edit the output, then save
Rate limit

AI generation is limited to 10 generations per hour per chapter to manage costs. Text is automatically capped at 5,000 characters.

Audio narration

Pages can also have auto-generated audio narration (powered by ElevenLabs). When enabled, a play button appears on the public page so visitors can listen to the content. Audio is generated in the chapter’s configured language.

Managing Events

Chapter admins and content creators can manage events at /admin/chapter/events.

  • Create event — title, start date/time, end date/time, location, description, and a published toggle.
  • Edit event — update any field at any time.
  • Delete event — permanently removes the event.

Published events appear on your chapter’s website and can be included in AI-generated content.

Chapter Coach Approvals

Chapter admins can review coaches who have submitted their profile for their chapter at /admin/chapter/coaches. The workflow is the same as global approvals:

  • Approve — publishes the coach to the directory and regenerates search embeddings.
  • Reject — with an optional reason sent by email notification.

Chapter Settings

At /admin/chapter/settings, chapter admins can update:

  • Chapter name
  • Region — Americas, Europe, Asia-Pacific, Africa, Middle East
  • Country
  • Language — English, Spanish, Portuguese, French
  • Contact email and phone
  • Description
  • Logo URL — custom chapter logo

Revenue Dashboard

Chapter admins can access a revenue dashboard at /account/revenue to monitor chapter dues and payment activity. This dashboard shows payment trends and financial summaries for the chapter.

Registering as a Coach

Any user with an account can register as a coach:

  1. Sign in to your account
  2. Navigate to /account/register-coach (or click "Register as coach" in your sidebar)
  3. Your role is immediately upgraded to coach
  4. You gain access to the certification courses, directory profile, and payment dues sections

Coach Profile

Coaches manage their directory profile at /dashboard/profile. Your profile includes:

  • Display name, email, phone
  • Location — city, country (with coordinates for map features)
  • Bio — a description of your coaching practice
  • Specializations — tags describing your areas of expertise
  • Languages — languages you coach in
  • Photo URL
  • Website and LinkedIn links
  • Credly badge URL — linked automatically if Credly integration is active
  • Audio intro — an AI-generated or uploaded audio introduction

After updating your profile, it goes through an approval process. A chapter admin or platform admin must approve changes before they appear in the public directory.

Dues and Payments

Coaches can manage their dues at /account/dues. The payment flow:

  1. View outstanding dues and their descriptions
  2. Click Pay now to create a Stripe checkout session
  3. Complete payment on Stripe’s secure checkout page
  4. You are redirected back to the platform on success

Payment history is available on the same page.

Becoming a Chapter Head

Coaches who want to lead a chapter can apply at /account/register-chapter-head. This upgrade requires:

  1. You must already be a coach
  2. A platform admin must assign you to a chapter first
  3. Once assigned, you can self-promote to chapter admin

Coach Directory

The public coach directory at /coaches lets anyone search for and discover WIAL coaches using a hybrid search system:

  • Free text search — type a name, specialization, or natural-language query like "leadership coaches in Japan who speak Spanish"
  • Filters — narrow results by certification level, country, city, language, and specializations
  • AI-powered parsing — complex queries are parsed by an LLM to extract structured filters automatically

The search combines four strategies behind the scenes:

  1. Vector search — semantic similarity using Cohere embeddings & pgvector
  2. Keyword search — PostgreSQL full-text search
  3. LLM query parsing — extracts structured filters from natural language
  4. Name fallback — direct name matching

Results are cached for 5 minutes and rate-limited per IP address.

Certification Hub (Public)

The public certification page at /certification provides an overview of all four certification tracks with requirements, application documents, and LMS links. This is a view-only resource for prospective and current coaches.

AI Chatbot

The site chatbot (available on public pages) answers questions about WIAL certification, requirements, upcoming events, and general Action Learning information. It uses GPT-4o-mini with a focused context window and is limited to 20 messages per minute per visitor.

Accessibility

The platform includes built-in accessibility options that visitors can adjust:

  • Text size — default, large, or extra-large
  • Contrast — default or high contrast mode
  • Reduced motion — disables animations for users who prefer less motion

These preferences are stored locally in the browser and applied automatically on return visits.

How Subdomains Work

Every chapter gets its own subdomain (e.g., usa.wial.org, japan.wial.org). When a visitor loads a chapter’s subdomain, the platform:

  1. Extracts the subdomain from the request
  2. Looks up the matching chapter (cached for 60 seconds)
  3. Injects tenant context headers into the request
  4. Rewrites the URL to the internal tenant route

This means chapter admins and visitors see clean URLs like usa.wial.org/about while the system internally routes them to the correct chapter content.

Local development

During development, you can access chapters via usa.localhost:3000 or japan.localhost:3000. Most browsers resolve *.localhost to 127.0.0.1 automatically.

Content Inheritance

Chapter sites inherit global content by default. If a chapter does not have a custom version of a page (e.g., /about), it will display the global version. When a chapter creates its own version of a page, that override takes priority on the chapter’s subdomain.

This means new chapters start with complete, professional content from day one and can progressively customize their site over time.

Troubleshooting

"No chapter workspace selected"

This means you are not assigned to a chapter. Ask a platform admin to assign your account to a chapter, or access your chapter through its subdomain URL.

AI content generation fails

Check that the OPENROUTER_API_KEY environment variable is set. AI features are disabled without it. Also confirm you have not exceeded the 10 generations/hour limit.

Coach not appearing in directory

Coach profiles must be approved by a chapter admin or platform admin before they appear in the public directory. Check the approvals queue.

Payments not working

Ensure STRIPE_SECRET_KEY is configured. In development, use Stripe’s test mode keys.

Subdomain not resolving locally

In development, use usa.localhost:3000 format. Most modern browsers resolve *.localhost to 127.0.0.1. If yours does not, add entries to /etc/hosts:

127.0.0.1  usa.localhost
127.0.0.1  japan.localhost

Email notifications not sending

Configure the RESEND_API_KEY environment variable. Without it, the platform skips email notifications for coach approvals, rejections, and welcome messages.

Audio narration missing

Audio generation requires the ELEVENLABS_API_KEY environment variable. Page content is also capped at 5,000 characters for audio generation.
